Internal Medicine

Internal Medicine focuses on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of adult diseases. Internists are experts in managing complex and chronic conditions, making them essential in long-term healthcare for adults.

Scope of Internal Medicine

  • Hypertension and Heart Disease
  • Diabetes Management
  • Respiratory Illnesses
  • Chronic Kidney Disease
  • Thyroid and Hormonal Disorders
  • General Preventive Care
Diabetes: Internists help patients manage both Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes through medications, lifestyle guidance, and regular blood sugar monitoring.

Cardiovascular Health

Internists play a key role in preventing and managing heart conditions such as high blood pressure, coronary artery disease, and cholesterol issues through early detection and ongoing care.

Respiratory Conditions

Internal medicine covers chronic respiratory issues like asthma, bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Proper management can greatly improve quality of life.

Preventive Screenings

Internists perform routine screenings for cancer, metabolic disorders, and infectious diseases. Annual physicals help detect health risks before symptoms arise.

Chronic Disease Management

Many adults suffer from multiple long-term conditions. Internal medicine provides continuous care and coordination across specialties for optimal patient outcomes.

When to See an Internist

Visit an internist if you need general adult healthcare, chronic disease management, or preventive screening. Internists often become long-term partners in maintaining your health.
