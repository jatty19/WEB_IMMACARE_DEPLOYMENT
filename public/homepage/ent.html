ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat)

ENT (Otolaryngology) is a medical specialty focused on the diagnosis and treatment of conditions related to the ears, nose, throat, head, and neck. Our ENT specialists provide care for both common and complex disorders affecting hearing, breathing, voice, and balance.

Conditions We Treat

  • Ear Infections and Hearing Loss
  • Sinusitis and Nasal Congestion
  • Tonsillitis and Sore Throat
  • Vertigo and Balance Issues
  • Snoring and Sleep Apnea
  • Voice and Swallowing Disorders
Sinusitis: Inflammation of the sinuses that causes congestion, headaches, and facial pain. Treatments include medications, nasal sprays, and sometimes surgical procedures.

Pediatric ENT Care

Children often experience ear infections, tonsillitis, or speech delays related to ENT issues. We provide gentle and specialized care to diagnose and treat young patients effectively.

Hearing and Balance Disorders

From age-related hearing loss to sudden vertigo, our ENT team offers advanced diagnostics such as audiometry and vestibular tests, as well as hearing aid fitting and surgical solutions.

Voice and Swallowing

Hoarseness, vocal strain, and difficulty swallowing can be signs of underlying ENT issues. We use video laryngoscopy and other tools to diagnose vocal cord problems and swallowing disorders.

When to See an ENT Specialist

Consult an ENT if you have persistent sinus problems, hearing loss, frequent throat infections, or difficulty speaking or swallowing. Early treatment prevents complications and improves quality of life.
